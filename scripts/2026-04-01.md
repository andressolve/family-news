And in the United States, the House passed a stopgap funding bill for the Department of Homeland Security after Republicans rejected a Senate deal, extending yet another budget standoff.

## Markets and Business

Markets are starting April on an optimistic note. S and P five hundred futures are up about half a percent, crossing sixty-six hundred. The Nasdaq is leading the way, up six tenths of a percent, with tech stocks driving the rally. Investors seem to be looking past March's volatility and focusing on cooling inflation signals and what could be a strong spring earnings season. Oil remains elevated but dipped slightly, with crude trading just above one hundred dollars a barrel. On the earnings calendar today, Conagra reports third quarter results. For anyone in the food industry, worth watching how they talk about input costs and pricing power in this environment. Over in India, the Nifty fifty and Sensex both surged over two percent on the first day of their new financial year, led by banking and IT stocks.

## Technology

OpenAI has crossed twenty-five billion dollars in annualized revenue and is reportedly taking early steps toward a public listing, potentially as soon as late this year. Anthropic, the company behind Claude, is approaching nineteen billion in annualized revenue. The AI revenue race is very real. On the product side, Google released Gemini three point one Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused model that delivers two and a half times faster response times at just twenty-five cents per million input tokens. And Nvidia announced a partnership with Alpamayo, an AI-driven simulation company, to accelerate autonomous vehicle development using high-fidelity digital twin environments. The goal is to reduce the cost and time of physical road testing by running more scenarios virtually.

## One More Thing

The whole heist took under three minutes before the alarm chased them off. The museum kept it secret for a week, hoping the thieves might come back. They did not.
