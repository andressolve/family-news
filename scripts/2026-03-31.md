It's Tuesday, March thirty-first, twenty twenty-six. Good morning. Here's what's happening today.

## World and Politics

The Iran conflict may be approaching a turning point. The Wall Street Journal reported yesterday that President Trump has signaled willingness to end the US military campaign. Markets reacted immediately to the headline. But the situation remains volatile. Trump also threatened to destroy Iran's Kharg Island, which handles ninety percent of Iran's oil exports, and suggested the US could simply take Iran's oil. Iran's new Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei, who took power after his father was killed in the initial strikes on February twenty-eighth, has rejected all US proposals so far.

The humanitarian toll continues to mount. In Lebanon, an Israeli airstrike hit Ramlet al-Baida beach in central Beirut yesterday, killing eight people and injuring thirty-one. Hezbollah responded with over two hundred rockets and twenty drones fired at northern Israel. Prime Minister Netanyahu announced plans to expand the ground invasion of Lebanon. The Houthis continue launching missiles at Israel as well.

Domestically, TSA workers have now gone over forty days without pay. President Trump says he has a plan to pay them, but hasn't provided details.

## Markets and Business

It's the last trading day of the first quarter, and futures are pointing higher. S and P five hundred futures are up about three-quarters of a percent. Dow futures are up three hundred sixty points. The bounce is driven by that Wall Street Journal report suggesting Trump may end the Iran campaign.

But it's been a brutal quarter. The S and P five hundred closed yesterday at sixty-three forty-three, down almost nine percent from its high. The Nasdaq is in correction territory, down over thirteen percent. Monday saw the VIX top thirty for the first time since twenty twenty-two.

Oil remains the key variable. Brent is near one hundred eight dollars. West Texas Intermediate settled above one hundred two dollars yesterday, its highest since July. Fed Chair Powell spoke at Harvard yesterday and said rates are in a good place, noting that by the time a rate change would take effect, the oil shock would likely be over. Translation: don't expect rate moves in response to energy prices.

Nike reports earnings after the bell today. Micron has fallen thirty percent in the last eight trading days after a Google breakthrough raised questions about future chip demand.

## Technology

France's Mistral secured eight hundred thirty million dollars in debt financing to buy nearly fourteen thousand Nvidia chips and build a major data center near Paris. They're targeting two hundred megawatts of compute across Europe by end of twenty twenty-seven. This is the European AI sovereignty play in action.

A startup called Starcloud raised one hundred seventy million dollars at a one-point-one billion dollar valuation for an unusual idea: AI data centers in space. Their plan is an eighty-eight thousand satellite constellation running compute workloads on near-continuous solar power. AI's next bottleneck is increasingly about power, cooling, and land, not just chips.

Reddit is rolling out new anti-bot policies starting today. Automated accounts will be labeled, human verification is expanding, and reporting tools are getting upgraded. Eighty-five percent of startups at the latest Y Combinator demo day were AI-first, the highest proportion in YC history.

## Francisco and Sebastian's Corner

Francisco, Sebastian, tomorrow is the day. Artemis Two launches tomorrow at six twenty-four PM Eastern time. The countdown officially started yesterday and everything is on track. Weather is eighty percent favorable. The four astronauts, Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Canada's Jeremy Hansen, will fly around the Moon and back over ten days. No human has been beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo seventeen in nineteen seventy-two. If you can, watch the launch live on NASA's YouTube channel. Coverage starts at seven forty-five AM Eastern with tanking operations.

On gaming, Legends of the Round Table launched this week. The hand-drawn art was made using actual thirteenth-century illumination techniques, and the music was recorded on period instruments. The combat is card-based. Also, if you haven't tried Hollow Knight Silksong yet, it hit Game Pass earlier this month and the reviews are excellent. It's a metroidvania where you play as Hornet exploring a kingdom ruled by silk and song.

## One More Thing

Exiled Iranian crown prince Reza Pahlavi, son of the last Shah who was overthrown in nineteen seventy-nine, said this week that he is ready to lead a transition if the current Iranian government falls. Whether that's realistic or not, the fact that it's being discussed openly tells you how dramatically this conflict has reshuffled what people think is possible in Iran.
