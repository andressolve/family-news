It's Sunday, April fifth, twenty twenty-six. Happy Easter. Here's what's happening today.

## World and Politics

Pope Leo the Fourteenth delivered his first Easter message this morning from Saint Peter's Square, urging those with weapons to lay them down and those with the power to start wars to choose peace. In his Urbi et Orbi blessing, the Pope addressed what he called the cry of pain rising from every corner of the world, pointing to the idolatry of profit plundering the earth's resources and the violence of war that kills and destroys. He spoke directly about the tombs still to be opened today, mistrust, fear, selfishness, and the isolation of nations.

The Iran war enters its sixth week. President Trump announced early this morning that a US Air Force officer whose plane was shot down over Iran on Friday has been rescued by US forces. Meanwhile, Israel has launched a ground invasion into southern Lebanon, pursuing Hezbollah fighters who have been firing rockets in support of Iran. Dozens of countries joined a virtual meeting yesterday to discuss reopening the Strait of Hormuz. Carriers including Air New Zealand, Vietnam Airlines, and Korean Air are cutting flights and entering emergency management mode as aviation fuel costs keep climbing.

In Cuba, the government announced it has pardoned and released over two thousand prisoners, a sweeping move as the island faces pressure from the Trump administration.

## Markets and Business

Markets were closed Friday for Good Friday. Before the break, the March jobs report came in stronger than expected at a hundred seventy-eight thousand new jobs, beating all estimates. That's a sign of labor market resilience despite the oil shock. But the S and P five hundred still closed its worst first quarter since twenty twenty-two.

Oil is where the real action is. Brent crude is holding above one hundred nine dollars a barrel. US gasoline prices are above four dollars a gallon nationally. The Strait of Hormuz blockade is now the largest oil supply disruption in history. Trump's primetime speech on Wednesday, promising two to three more weeks of strikes, didn't calm anything.

Looking ahead to Monday, traders will react to the jobs report and digest weekend developments on the war. Tesla reported first-quarter deliveries up year over year but down sequentially, and shares closed down five percent on the week. Intel bought back its forty-nine percent stake in its Irish fab from Apollo for over fourteen billion dollars.

## Technology

The big story this week was a supply chain attack on an open-source AI library called LiteLLM, used to hit an AI startup called Mercor. It's the clearest example yet of how open-source components in the AI stack are becoming attractive targets for sophisticated attackers. Expect a wave of AI-specific security audits and compliance requirements in the coming months.

OpenAI acquired the tech podcast network TBPN, moving further into the creator economy. Cognichip raised sixty million dollars to build AI tools for designing semiconductor chips. And Nvidia announced a partnership with Alpamayo to build hyper-realistic simulation environments for autonomous vehicles, using the DRIVE Orin and Thor platforms.

On the policy front, a new AI legislation tracker shows forty-seven US states now have active AI bills, up from twelve a year ago. State-level regulation is growing faster than any federal framework.

## Francisco and Sebastian's Corner

Francisco, Sebastian, Artemis Two has its big moment tomorrow. The four astronauts are currently on flight day five and preparing for the lunar flyby. Tomorrow afternoon, starting around two forty-five PM Eastern, the spacecraft will pass within just seventy-eight hundred miles of the Moon's surface. At seven oh five PM Eastern, they'll reach their maximum distance from Earth, over two hundred fifty-two thousand seven hundred miles. That beats the Apollo thirteen distance record by four thousand one hundred two miles. That will make them the farthest human beings have ever traveled. They'll photograph the far side of the Moon, the side no human has ever seen directly with their own eyes. Worth watching live on NASA TV.

In gaming, Pokemon Champions launches Wednesday, a competitive turn-based strategy game on Switch, Switch Two, and mobile with full crossplay. And Hades Two hits PlayStation and Xbox on April fourteenth. The original was one of the best action roguelikes ever made.

## One More Thing

Hubble Space Telescope scientists released a new image this week of a comet breaking apart in real time. They called it a lucky cosmic accident that astronomers have tried and failed to observe for decades. Comets occasionally shatter as they travel through space, but catching it mid-shatter with a powerful telescope is vanishingly rare.
