## World and Politics

The Iran conflict hit its one-month mark yesterday. Iran struck Prince Sultan Air Base in Saudi Arabia, injuring at least ten US service members. Two had shrapnel wounds but none were killed. Separately, Iran launched missiles at Israel overnight. Footage from Tel Aviv shows damaged buildings, with several impact sites reported across the district. Israel intercepted a missile from Yemen as well.

The Strait of Hormuz remains the central pressure point. Before the war, a hundred thirty ships transited daily. Now it's essentially shut down, blocking twenty percent of the world's oil and liquefied natural gas supply. President Trump extended his deadline for Iran to reopen the strait to April sixth, saying talks are going well. Secretary of State Rubio is in France meeting G7 allies to shore up support.

Kuwait International Airport is still closed after the drone strike earlier this week. No reopening date has been announced. And in Lebanon, the death toll from Israeli strikes since March second has reached eleven hundred forty-two, with twenty percent of the population displaced. UNICEF says there is no safe space left in the country.

## Markets and Business

It was a brutal week on Wall Street. The Dow fell seven hundred ninety-three points on Friday, dropping into correction territory alongside the Nasdaq, which is now down thirteen percent from its October high. The S and P five hundred closed at sixty-three sixty-eight, down one-point-seven percent. All three indexes posted their fifth straight weekly decline, the longest losing streak since twenty twenty-two.

Oil is the story. Brent crude topped a hundred ten dollars a barrel after new incidents in the Strait of Hormuz. The ten-year Treasury yield hit four-point-four-one percent, its highest since July twenty twenty-five. Rising yields plus rising oil is the stagflation signal that markets fear most.

The government shutdown is adding pressure. TSA officers have been working without pay for weeks, causing four-hour security delays at some airports. DHS says paychecks should arrive by Monday. Bitcoin dropped to sixty-six thousand, down nearly twenty-five percent year to date.

And the US Treasury Department launched an AI Innovation Series, a public-private initiative to prepare the financial system for accelerating AI adoption.
