It's Monday, March thirtieth, twenty twenty-six. Good morning. Here's what's happening today.

## World and Politics

Day thirty of the Iran conflict. President Trump said yesterday that Iran has agreed to most of the US fifteen-point plan to end the war. But Tehran is calling that characterization misleading, with Foreign Minister Araghchi saying messages have been exchanged through intermediaries but no formal agreement has been reached. Pakistan announced it will host direct US-Iran talks in the coming days, and a meeting of foreign ministers from Pakistan, Saudi Arabia, Turkey, and Egypt took place in Islamabad over the weekend to push de-escalation.

On the military front, Trump said he's considering seizing Iran's Kharg Island, which handles ninety percent of Iran's oil exports. Iran's parliament speaker warned that any ground invasion would be met with force. The Houthis have now entered the conflict, launching missiles at Israel over the weekend. A UN peacekeeper was killed in southern Lebanon. And domestically, a new Pew survey shows fifty-nine percent of Americans say the military action in Iran was the wrong decision.

Pope Leo the Fourteenth used his Palm Sunday message to reject claims that God justifies war, praying specifically for Christians in the Middle East.

## Markets and Business

It was the worst week for stocks since twenty twenty-two. The Dow fell into correction territory on Friday, dropping seven hundred ninety-three points to close at forty-five thousand one hundred sixty-six. The S and P five hundred hit a seven-month low. The Nasdaq dropped over two percent and is now almost thirteen percent below its October record. That's five straight weekly declines.

Futures are mixed this morning. It's a holiday-shortened week with markets closed Friday for Good Friday. The big data point is the March jobs report, which lands on Friday but markets won't be able to react until next Monday.

Oil continues to drive everything. Brent crude topped one hundred twelve dollars on Friday. The Strait of Hormuz blockade is now the biggest oil supply shock in history according to the IEA. The ten-year Treasury yield hit four point four-eight percent, and the thirty-year briefly touched five percent. Australia is offering free public transit in two states to ease the fuel price burden, and is considering nationwide fuel rationing.

## Technology

OpenAI quietly shut down the Sora public API last week, giving developers thirty days notice. The reason: unsustainable economics of video generation at scale. That's a notable admission from a company valued at hundreds of billions of dollars.

In AI security, both Microsoft and Visa are hiring senior directors specifically for AI security roles, responding to growing threats from model inversion attacks and prompt injection in production environments. New audit standards now require SOC two compliance specifically for model weights and training data.

Google released Gemini three-point-one Flash Lite, an efficiency-focused model that's two and a half times faster and costs just twenty-five cents per million input tokens. And from China, MiniMax released its M two-point-five model, which reviewers say rivals Claude Opus at significantly lower cost.

On the policy front, Trump is forming a twenty-four-member AI council co-chaired by David Sacks, with Zuckerberg, Ellison, and Huang as members.

## Francisco and Sebastian's Corner

Francisco, Sebastian, the Artemis Two countdown begins today. NASA's launch countdown starts this afternoon at three PM Eastern, targeting liftoff on Wednesday at six twenty-four PM. If it goes, it will be the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo seventeen in nineteen seventy-two. Victor Glover will become the first person of color to travel beyond low Earth orbit, Christina Koch the first woman, and Jeremy Hansen from Canada the first non-American. The spacecraft will travel over two hundred fifty-two thousand miles from Earth, farther than any Apollo crew ever went. Weather forecast is eighty percent favorable.

On the gaming front, this is the last day to grab the free Amazon Prime games. Total War Rome Two Emperor Edition and Total War Three Kingdoms are both still available. Three Kingdoms in particular is considered one of the best modern Total War games. And coming to Xbox this week, Legacy of Kain Ascendance launches tomorrow, a dark fantasy action platformer set in Nosgoth.

## One More Thing

Iran's parliament is debating whether to formally withdraw from the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty, which would make it only the second country ever to do so after North Korea in two thousand three. If it happens, it would fundamentally change the global nonproliferation framework that has held for over fifty years.
