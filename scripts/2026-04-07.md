It's Tuesday, April seventh, twenty twenty-six. Good morning. Here's what's happening today.

## World and Politics

The Middle East conflict enters its thirty-eighth day with no signs of slowing. Iran has rejected a forty-five day ceasefire proposal from the United States, and President Trump's deadline for Iran to reopen the Strait of Hormuz is approaching with no deal in sight. Overnight, unconfirmed reports emerged of US strikes on military targets on Kharg Island, Iran's main oil export terminal. Iran's Supreme Leader Mojtaba Khamenei struck a defiant tone, saying Iran's forces would not be deterred by the assassinations of military commanders. Meanwhile, Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu is reportedly urging Trump not to agree to any ceasefire deal. In Gaza, an Israeli airstrike killed at least ten people as the Hamas ceasefire continues to crumble. And in Lebanon, the Israeli invasion has displaced over a million civilians, with more than thirteen hundred killed according to Lebanon's government. The humanitarian toll across the region continues to mount.

## Markets and Business

Markets are jittery heading into the week. Futures fell Monday evening after reports of the Kharg Island strikes, with Dow futures shedding two hundred five points. In early trading, the S and P five hundred edged up a tenth of a percent and the Nasdaq gained four tenths, but the mood remains fragile. The big number to watch is oil. West Texas Intermediate crude climbed two percent to a hundred and fifteen dollars a barrel. That price has enormous downstream effects. Before the conflict started, airlines had forecast record forty-one billion dollars in profits this year. Now, with jet fuel costs more than doubling, carriers are scrambling. For anyone in the food industry, energy and transport costs are working through the supply chain fast. This Friday brings the March CPI report, the first major data release expected to capture early inflation impacts from the conflict. Economists are forecasting headline inflation rose a full one percent last month, up from three tenths in February.

## Technology

A research team has unveiled a new approach to AI that combines neural networks with symbolic reasoning, cutting energy consumption by up to a hundred times while actually improving accuracy. Instead of brute-force trial and error, the system helps robots reason more logically. It's a promising step toward making AI sustainable at scale. In industry news, OpenAI has surpassed twenty-five billion dollars in annualized revenue and is reportedly exploring a public listing as soon as late this year. The company's recent hundred and twenty-two billion dollar fundraise valued it at eight hundred and fifty-two billion. Google introduced Gemini three point one Flash-Lite, an efficiency-focused model that delivers two and a half times faster response times. And Apple CarPlay now supports voice-based interaction with ChatGPT through the latest iOS update.
